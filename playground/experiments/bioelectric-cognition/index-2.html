The Dual-Threshold Insight

Novel Discovery: Bioelectric networks exhibit TWO critical thresholds, not one. Below the lower threshold (GJ_min), cells fragment into isolated "voltage islands" with independent polarization. Above the upper threshold (GJ_max), excessive connectivity short-circuits voltage gradients, erasing positional information.

The Narrowing Corridor: As organism size increases (more cells), the viable parameter corridor between these thresholds shrinks as a fraction of total parameter space. This creates a body-size ceiling for morphogenetic competency without proportionally increased gap junction regulation precision.

Biological Implication: This predicts why highly regenerative organisms (planaria, axolotls) tend to be small to medium-sized, while large mammals have diminished regenerative capacity. The dual-threshold channel narrows allometrically with cell count.

Engineering Application: Scaling up bioengineered constructs (organoids, anthrobots) will encounter a predictable wall where bioelectric regulation becomes insufficient unless gap junction precision is simultaneously increased.
